Social Media Investment Fraud

Social media investment frauds have risen dramatically in India, with scammers using platforms like Instagram, Facebook, and WhatsApp to lure victims with promises of high returns.

Warning Signs

  • Unusually high returns (40-50% monthly)
  • Pressure to recruit others (pyramid scheme)
  • Requests to download unknown apps
  • Foreign bank account transfers

Prevention

  • Verify with SEBI before investing
  • Never invest based solely on social media advice
  • Be wary of schemes requiring you to recruit others

Source: I4C Report 2024, Business Standard

Impersonation Scams (Digital Arrests)

Impersonation scams involve fraudsters pretending to be government officials, police, or other authorities to frighten victims into making payments to avoid supposed legal consequences.

Warning Signs

  • Callers claiming to be from CBI, Income Tax, or Police
  • Threats of immediate arrest or legal action
  • Requests for immediate payment to avoid arrest
  • Video calls showing people in fake uniforms

Prevention

  • No government agency demands payment over phone
  • Never share personal information over phone
  • Report suspicious calls to 1930

Source: I4C Report 2024, Business Standard

UPI/Banking Scams

UPI scams are among the most common in India due to the widespread adoption of digital payment platforms like Google Pay, PhonePe, and Paytm.

Warning Signs

  • Requests to scan QR codes to receive money
  • Calls asking for UPI PIN or OTP verification
  • Fake customer care numbers found on Google
  • Requests to install screen sharing apps

Prevention

  • Never scan QR codes to receive money
  • Never share OTP, PIN, or CVV with anyone
  • Verify merchant UPI IDs before payment
  • Use official apps and customer care numbers only

Source: I4C Report 2024, RBI Advisory

Online Auction Scams

Online auction scams involve fraudulent listings on e-commerce platforms, social media marketplaces, or fake auction websites that never deliver products after payment.

Warning Signs

  • Prices far below market value
  • Sellers refusing to meet in person
  • Requests for advance payments via UPI
  • Limited communication options

Prevention

  • Use established platforms with buyer protection
  • Research seller ratings and reviews
  • Avoid advance payments without seeing products
  • Meet in safe public places for high-value transactions

Source: I4C Report 2024, Consumer Affairs Ministry
